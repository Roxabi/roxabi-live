## Context

Part of the Lyra Origin Talks trilogy. `/talks/lyra-dev` (#450) is done. This is the second
talk — same 52-day journey, but told through product/startup decision-making: wrong bets,
iteration speed, killing darlings, ecosystem emergence.

Route: `/talks/lyra-product`. Source material: `histoire-2ndbrain-lyra.md`.
Pattern reference: `apps/web/src/components/presentation/lyra-dev/`.

## Goal

Deliver a scroll-snap presentation at `/talks/lyra-product` — 18 sections, i18n FR/EN — that
tells the 52-day story (2ndBrain → Lyra ecosystem) to a startup/product/indie-hacker audience
through the lens of product decisions and iteration velocity.

## Users

- **Primary:** Startup founders, indie hackers, product-minded devs at meetups/conferences.
  They care about: decisions, speed, killing features, ecosystem thinking.
- **Secondary:** Anyone on the product/build side who's pivoted or watched a side project
  outgrow its original form.

## Expected Behavior

A visitor navigating to `/talks/lyra-product` sees a full-screen dark-warm presentation.
Sections snap on scroll (desktop). The Lyra avatar companion tracks progress in the corner.
Nav dots on the right allow jumping between sections. Keyboard arrows + Escape (→ `/talks`) work.
Locale switcher (FR/EN) and theme toggle live in the top-right. A warm amber/orange accent
palette distinguishes this talk visually from `lyra-dev` (emerald terminal) and `lyra-story`
(neutral theme-aware).

Each section tells one beat of the arc. Copy comes from `histoire-2ndbrain-lyra.md`. A
`SectionChrome` decorates each section with a phase label + day counter (amber/orange/cyan/rose
depending on phase). No mode toggle — single narrative mode throughout.
